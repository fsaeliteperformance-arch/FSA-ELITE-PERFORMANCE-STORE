# 06 — Slide Deck Outline

> **Usage:** Use this outline to build slide decks for:
> - Webinars and live training sessions
> - Corporate sales pitches (B2B / team licence proposals)
> - Conference presentations
> - Micro-Coaching group session decks
>
> Build in Google Slides, Keynote, or PowerPoint.  
> Brand colours: Gold `#F59E0B` on Black `#0A0A0A`.

---

## Deck 1: Webinar / Live Training Deck
*Length: 45–60 minutes · Slides: 25–35*

### Section 1 — Hook (Slides 1–4)
| Slide | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Cover | Logo · Session title · Date · "Welcome — we start at [time]" |
| 2 | The Agenda | 5 bullet points of what they'll learn today |
| 3 | The Cost of Not Knowing This | Stat or story: "The average rep loses 3 deals per month to objections they could have handled." |
| 4 | Your Host | Brief credibility: who you are, why you're qualified to teach this |

### Section 2 — The Problem (Slides 5–8)
| Slide | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 5 | What Most Reps Do When They Hear an Objection | Wrong approach visualised (blank stare, "I'll follow up later") |
| 6 | The 12 Objections Every Rep Will Face | Visual: objection wheel or list |
| 7 | Why Generic Training Doesn't Work | Brief critique: generic scripts, no role-play, theory without application |
| 8 | What Actually Works | Preview: the FSA framework (tease, don't reveal yet) |

### Section 3 — The Framework (Slides 9–18)
| Slide | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 9 | The FEEL-FELT-FOUND Framework | Definition + visual 3-step diagram |
| 10 | Step 1: FEEL — Acknowledge | Script example + coaching note |
| 11 | Step 2: FELT — Normalise | Script example + coaching note |
| 12 | Step 3: FOUND — Pivot | Script example + coaching note |
| 13 | Live Example: "Not right now" | Before script vs. after script |
| 14 | Live Example: "Send me more info" | Before vs. after |
| 15 | Live Example: "We're already with [Competitor]" | Before vs. after |
| 16 | Live Example: "Your price is too high" | Before vs. after |
| 17 | Role-Play Clip #1 | [Embed video or describe scenario] |
| 18 | Role-Play Clip #2 | [Embed video or describe scenario] |

### Section 4 — Tools & Implementation (Slides 19–23)
| Slide | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 19 | Your Personal Objection Cheat Sheet | How to build it · QR code to downloadable template |
| 20 | The 5-Minute Daily Drill | Habit: one objection + response, every morning |
| 21 | Implementation Plan | Week 1, Week 2, Week 3 actions |
| 22 | What to Do in Your Next 24 Hours | 3 specific actions |
| 23 | Q&A | Open the floor |

### Section 5 — Close / CTA (Slides 24–26)
| Slide | Title | Content |
|---|---|---|
| 24 | Ready to Go Deeper? | Introduce Objection Masterclass ($197) |
| 25 | What You Get | Course contents, lifetime access, guarantee |
| 26 | Enrol Today | QR code / link to `STRIPE_PAYMENT_LINK_COURSE_01` · Offer code if applicable |

## Slide Design Notes

- **Background:** `#0A0A0A` (deep black)
- **Headlines:** White (`#F5F5F5`) · Font: Inter Bold or similar sans-serif
- **Accent / highlights:** FSA Gold `#F59E0B`
- **Body text:** `#AAAAAA`
- **One idea per slide** — no walls of text
- **Use visuals, numbers, and short scripts** — never long paragraphs
- **Every slide with a framework should include the word-for-word script**, not just the concept name
