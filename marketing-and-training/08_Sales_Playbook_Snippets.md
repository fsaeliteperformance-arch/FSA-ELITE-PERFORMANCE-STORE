# 08 — Sales Playbook Snippets

Ready-to-use script fragments and conversation starters for common sales situations.  
Copy, adapt, and practice these until they feel natural — then personalise for your style and industry.

---

## 1. Cold Call Openers

### Opener A — Permission-based (SDR)
> "Hey [Name], this is [Your Name] from FSA Elite. I know I'm catching you out of the blue — I'll be quick. Do you have 30 seconds?"

*Why it works:* Asking for permission disarms the "who is this?" reflex. Most people say yes.

---

### Opener B — Referral / Trigger-based (SDR / AE)
> "Hey [Name], I saw you just [trigger: hired 3 SDRs / posted about scaling your sales team / moved to a new role]. Congrats. That's actually why I'm calling — we help [role] at companies like [similar company] with [outcome]. Is now a bad time for 2 minutes?"

*Why it works:* Relevance. You're not interrupting — you have a reason.

---

### Opener C — Warm LinkedIn Connect Follow-up
> "Hey [Name], you connected with me on LinkedIn last week — thanks for that. I noticed you're [title] at [company]. I work with a lot of [similar roles] on [specific problem]. Would it be worth a quick 15-minute conversation to see if there's a fit?"

*Why it works:* Starts from a warm signal. Lower resistance than pure cold.

---

## 2. Discovery Questions

### Qualifying questions (MEDDIC-aligned)
> "Who else is involved in decisions like this one — is it just you, or are there others we'd need to bring in?"

> "What does success look like for you 90 days after implementing this?"

> "What happens if this problem doesn't get solved by [date]? Is there a cost to waiting?"

> "You mentioned [pain]. How long has that been an issue, and why now?"

> "If we can show you [outcome], what would the buying process look like on your end?"

---

### Uncovering the real objection
> "I get that. Just so I understand — is [stated reason] the main thing, or is there something else that would make this a harder decision?"

*Use this when the stated objection feels like a deflection.*

---

## 3. Objection Handling Scripts

### "Not right now / timing isn't right"
> "Completely understand — timing is always a factor. A lot of our clients felt the same way initially. What they found is that [specific ROI or outcome], so they actually saved time and cost by starting now vs. Q2. What would need to change for the timing to work?"

---

### "Send me more info"
> "Of course. Just so I send you the most relevant thing — what specifically would be most useful? And what would it take for that information to move things forward on your end?"

---

### "We're already working with [Competitor]"
> "That makes sense — [Competitor] does a good job with [specific area]. Can I ask: is there any area where you feel like there's a gap? Most of our customers who switched had been with [Competitor] and found that [specific differentiator] was what they were missing."

---

### "Your price is too high"
> "I hear you — budget is always a real constraint. Can I ask: is it that the number is outside your budget entirely, or that you're not yet sure the ROI justifies it? Those are two different problems and I want to make sure I'm solving the right one."

---

### "We need to think about it"
> "Of course — this is an important decision. What I've found is that 'think about it' usually means one of a few things: either there's a concern we haven't addressed yet, or it's genuinely not the right time. Which one is closer to where you are?"

---

## 4. Closing Scripts

### The Direct Close
> "Based on everything we've discussed, it sounds like this is a fit. The next step would be [specific action]. Does [specific date/time] work for you to move forward?"

---

### The Alternative Close
> "Are you thinking you'd want to start with the [Course/Subscription] on its own first, or does the Bundle make more sense for where you are right now?"

*Give two options — both move forward.*

---

### The Urgency Close (with a real reason)
> "The early-bird discount expires at [date/time]. If we get the paperwork done today, you lock in the lower rate. After that it goes back to full price. Does it make sense to move today?"

*Only use if the urgency is genuine — never manufacture false urgency.*

---

### The "Future Pace" Close
> "Imagine it's 90 days from now. Your team has used the playbook for a full quarter. What would need to have happened for you to look back and say this was the best investment you made this year?"

*Then listen. Their answer tells you exactly what they value — and whether they've already mentally bought.*

## 5. Follow-Up Messages

### 24-hour post-call follow-up (email)
**Subject:** Great speaking with you today — [next step]

---

Hi [Name],

Really enjoyed our conversation today. As promised:

→ [Resource / link / document you agreed to send]

Based on what we discussed, the most relevant option for you looks like [specific recommendation] because [specific reason tied to their pain].

I'll follow up on [specific date] about [specific next step]. In the meantime, feel free to reach out with any questions.

— [Your Name]

---

### 1-week no-reply follow-up
**Subject:** Still worth a conversation?

---

Hi [Name],

Following up from my email last week. I know things get busy.

One quick thought before I leave you alone: [one specific insight relevant to their situation, or a short case study that mirrors their pain].

Worth a 15-minute conversation? If not, totally fine — no hard feelings.

— [Your Name]

## 6. LinkedIn DM Templates

### First touch (after viewing profile or post)
> "Hey [Name] — saw your post about [topic]. Really resonated. I help [role] at [similar companies] with [outcome]. Would it make sense to connect and swap notes?"

---

### After connecting (soft CTA)
> "Thanks for connecting, [Name]. I notice you're [building/scaling/leading] [function] at [company]. We've been helping [similar companies] with [specific problem]. Would a quick call make sense, or would you rather I share something useful first?"

---

### Post-content engagement
> "Great point in your comment about [topic]. That's exactly the challenge [similar companies] bring to us. Happy to share how we've seen it solved — interested?"
